3. The Concept — Transmission-Based Precautions

Standard vs Transmission-Based Precautions

Standard precautions apply to every patient, every time — hand hygiene, PPE when body fluids may contact skin or mucous membranes, safe injection practices, and respiratory hygiene. Transmission-based precautions are added on top when a known or suspected pathogen requires extra isolation beyond standard precautions.

There are exactly three transmission-based types on NCLEX:

TypePathogen examplesHow it spreadsRoom / PPE
Contact MRSA, C. diff, scabies, wound drainage Direct touch or contaminated surfaces/equipment Gown + gloves for all room entry; private room if possible
Droplet Influenza, pertussis, meningococcal meningitis, mumps Large respiratory droplets — travel ~3–6 feet, do not linger in air Surgical mask within 3–6 ft; patient may share room with same organism
Airborne TB, measles, varicella (chickenpox), disseminated zoster Tiny droplet nuclei — remain suspended, travel on air currents Negative-pressure room (AIIR); N95 or higher respirator; door closed

Negative-Pressure Rooms (Airborne Infection Isolation)

An AIIR (airborne infection isolation room) keeps air pressure lower inside the room than outside so air flows in when the door opens — contaminated air does not escape into the hallway. The door stays closed. Only staff with fit-tested N95 (or PAPR) enter.

Patient Transport Off the Unit

Isolated patients still need imaging, procedures, and tests. The nurse must:

  • Cover draining or infected sites with clean, impermeable dressings
  • Notify transport and receiving department of precaution type
  • Ensure receiving staff have appropriate PPE before handoff
  • Never discontinue precautions without provider/order and infection control clearance
NCLEX trap: Influenza is droplet, not airborne — surgical mask, not N95. Measles and varicella are airborne — N95 required. C. diff is contact — soap and water hand hygiene preferred over alcohol gel.

Connection to NCLEX-RN Exam

Isolation questions appear as standalone items and inside case studies. The exam tests whether you know the most restrictive precaution required when organisms differ, and whether you select correct PPE before patient contact — not after.
