3. The Concept — PPE Selection & Donning/Doffing

Why PPE Sequence Matters

Personal protective equipment creates barriers between you and infectious material. The order you don (put on) and doff (remove) PPE determines whether you contaminate your skin, scrubs, or the environment. NCLEX frequently tests doffing — removing PPE is when most self-contamination happens.

Donning Sequence (Put On)

  1. Perform hand hygiene
  2. Gown — fully cover torso, tie neck and waist
  3. Mask or respirator — secure fit; N95 requires seal check
  4. Eye protection — goggles or face shield if not integrated in mask
  5. Gloves — last; cuff over gown sleeves

Doffing Sequence (Remove) — NCLEX High-Yield

  1. Gloves — first; avoid touching outer surfaces
  2. Gown — roll away from body; only touch inside of gown
  3. Eye protection — handle by headband/straps
  4. Mask/respirator — do not touch front of mask
  5. Hand hygiene — immediately, before any other action

N95 vs Surgical Mask

ScenarioMask type
Airborne — TB, measles, varicellaN95 or PAPR (fit-tested)
Droplet — influenza, pertussisSurgical mask within 3–6 ft
Contact — MRSA, C. diffMask only if splash risk; gown + gloves required
Standard precautions — routine carePer exposure risk assessment
NCLEX trap: After doffing all PPE, hand hygiene is always next — not documentation, not re-donning gloves, not leaving the room. Touching the contaminated outer mask surface during removal is the highest-risk doffing error.
