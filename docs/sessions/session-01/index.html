2. Pre-Study Quiz

Answer these before reading the concept explanation or starting the study session. Score honestly. You need 4/5 (80%) to proceed.

Question 1 of 5

When should hand hygiene be performed according to standard precautions?

Standard precautions require hand hygiene before and after every patient contact, even when gloves are worn.
Question 2 of 5

A patient has confirmed MRSA wound infection. Which isolation precaution is required?

MRSA is spread by direct contact. Contact precautions include gown and gloves for all room entry.
Question 3 of 5

Which action breaks the chain of infection at the portal of entry?

Aseptic technique and wound care block pathogens from entering the body through broken skin or mucous membranes.
Question 4 of 5

A nurse removes gloves after wound care but does not perform hand hygiene before leaving the room. What is the primary risk?

Microorganisms on glove surfaces transfer to hands and then to the environment without hand hygiene.
Question 5 of 5

Standard precautions apply to:

Standard precautions treat all blood, body fluids, and mucous membranes as potentially infectious.

3. The Concept — Standard Precautions & Hand Hygiene

Clinical Foundation — Standard Precautions & Hand Hygiene

This session covers Hand hygiene timing, standard precautions, chain of infection. NCLEX-RN questions in the Infection Control & Safety domain test whether you can apply nursing knowledge to patient scenarios — not whether you can recite definitions.

Registered nurses must integrate assessment findings, evidence-based interventions, and safety protocols. Every question on the NCLEX expects you to think like a nurse at the bedside: assess first, prioritize by acuity, intervene safely, and evaluate outcomes.

Key Concepts for This Session

  • Core focus: Hand hygiene timing, standard precautions, chain of infection
  • Module context: Infection Control & Safety — Module 1 of 10
  • NCLEX category: Safe and Effective Care Environment / Physiological Integrity
  • Study milestone: Document one patient case with infection risk factors
NCLEX testing tip: When two answers seem correct, choose the one that addresses the most acute patient need first. Use ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation) and Maslow's hierarchy as your prioritization framework.

Clinical Application

In practice, standard precautions & hand hygiene requires the nurse to gather data, recognize patterns, and select interventions that prevent harm. On the NCLEX, distractors often include actions that are correct in isolation but wrong for the specific scenario — always read the question stem carefully for keywords like "first," "priority," "best," and "initial."
