3. The Concept — Triage & Multiple Patient Scenarios

Clinical Foundation — Triage & Multiple Patient Scenarios

This session covers Disaster triage tags, simultaneous demands. NCLEX-RN questions in the Patient Prioritization domain test whether you can apply nursing knowledge to patient scenarios — not whether you can recite definitions.

Registered nurses must integrate assessment findings, evidence-based interventions, and safety protocols. Every question on the NCLEX expects you to think like a nurse at the bedside: assess first, prioritize by acuity, intervene safely, and evaluate outcomes.

Key Concepts for This Session

  • Core focus: Disaster triage tags, simultaneous demands
  • Module context: Patient Prioritization — Module 4 of 10
  • NCLEX category: Safe and Effective Care Environment / Physiological Integrity
  • Study milestone: Triage 6 patients in ED waiting room
NCLEX testing tip: When two answers seem correct, choose the one that addresses the most acute patient need first. Use ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation) and Maslow's hierarchy as your prioritization framework.

Clinical Application

In practice, triage & multiple patient scenarios requires the nurse to gather data, recognize patterns, and select interventions that prevent harm. On the NCLEX, distractors often include actions that are correct in isolation but wrong for the specific scenario — always read the question stem carefully for keywords like "first," "priority," "best," and "initial."
